# KDE Connect cannot find your device

### First check this

Your phone and PC must be on the **same WiFi**.\
Also check the WiFi band. Both devices must use the **same band**, either 2.4 GHz or 5 GHz.

If that is correct, continue with the steps below.

***

### If it was working before and suddenly stopped

A clean reinstall often fixes it.

#### On Windows

1. Open the Windows search bar.
2. Type KDE Connect.
3. Right click.
4. Select Uninstall.\
   ![](/broken/files/1w7ltDELzqefQI1sDsrw)

#### On Android

1. Long press the KDE Connect app.
2. Tap Uninstall.
3. Install it again from the Play Store.

Then try pairing again.

***

### If you are using KDE Connect for the first time

#### Step 1. Set your Windows network to Private

1. Click the WiFi icon.
2. Select your network.
3. Click the info button.
4. Scroll to Network profile.
5.  Choose Private.\\

    <figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/image (1) (1) (1) (1).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

#### Step 2. Turn off Device Isolation on Windows

1. Open **Settings**.
2. Go to **Privacy and security**.
3. Select **Windows Security**.
4. Click **Open Windows Security**.
5. Go to **Device security**.
6. Open **Core isolation**.
7. Turn **Memory integrity** off.
8. Restart your PC.

{% embed url="https://iframe.mediadelivery.net/play/289332/ec9fecdc-1102-46fc-bfcb-f079b8d65acd" %}

#### Step 3. Disable Private DNS on Android

1. Open Settings.
2. Go to Network and Internet.
3. Tap Private DNS.
4. Set it to Off.

{% embed url="https://iframe.mediadelivery.net/play/289332/cd1810d1-b2ae-4681-b5a2-7c0b127731d8" %}

Restart your phone and try pairing again.
